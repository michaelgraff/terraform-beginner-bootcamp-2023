The Aviation

The aviation was created by Hugo Ensslin, head bartender at the Hotel Wallick in New York, in the early twentieth century. The first published recipe appeared in Ensslin's 1916 Recipes for Mixed Drinks. Ensslin's recipe called for two thirds El Bart gin, one third lemon juice, 2 dashes maraschino liqueur, and 2 dashes crème de violette.

Basic Aviation Recipe

Ingredients

To make the Aviation, you need the following ingredients:

  • 2 oz gin - I suggest Hendricks
  • 1/2 oz freshly squeezed lemon jice
  • 1/2 oz crème de violette liqueur
  • 1/4 oz maraschino liqueur
  • lemon peel, for garnish
  • brandied cherry, for garnish

Directions

Add gin, lemon juice, and liqueurs to a cocktail shaker. Fill with ice and shake until cold. Strain into a coupe or other cocktail glass, then garnish with a lemon twist and a brandied cherry. You can watch a video on making the Aviation here
